Personalised Stock Portfolio Recommender

A cluster-then-rank recommender that maps a five-question risk questionnaire to a 5 to 10 stock portfolio drawn from the S&P 500 plus DAX 40 universe, with a hard 30% per-sector cap.

Motivation

A retail investor who wants to start picking individual stocks usually has two options: pay for a broker's recommendation, or open an online screener and stare at hundreds of tickers without knowing where to begin. Existing tools either force every user into a generic "aggressive / moderate / conservative" bucket or assume the user already knows the vocabulary of finance.

This project specifies a recommender that sits between those two extremes: it asks five questions (years of investing experience, time horizon, drawdown tolerance, monthly budget, sector preferences) and returns a small portfolio of 5 to 10 stocks with the risk metrics that justify each choice. The output is decision support for a beginner investor, not financial advice.

Disclaimer. The Streamlit demo (v1.0) shows a prominent disclaimer: this tool produces information for education and decision support, it is not a registered investment advisor and the user is responsible for any trades made on its output.

Data sources

  • Universe: S&P 500 constituents + DAX 40 constituents from Wikipedia (~540 tickers after deduplication).
  • Daily OHLCV: 5 years of history via yfinance with auto_adjust=True so closes already absorb splits and dividends.
  • Risk-free rate: FRED DGS10 (10-year US Treasury), averaged over the window.
  • Fundamentals (advanced model): yf.Ticker.info for trailing P/E and dividend yield, with Alpha Vantage free tier as backup.
  • Offline fallback: Kaggle "Huge Stock Market Dataset" only if Yahoo rate-limits the entire run.

No bulk pre-download is shipped: yfinance fetches the full universe in roughly 90 seconds and caches it as parquet under data/cache/. See data/README.md for exact fetch commands.

Methods - cluster, rank, cap

STEP 1

Cluster

K-Means on five features (annualised return, vol, Sharpe, beta, max drawdown). k chosen by silhouette over k in {3..10}. Advanced variant uses 16-dim autoencoder embeddings of the weekly-return surface.

STEP 2

Rank

Within each cluster: trailing Sharpe (baseline) or LightGBM lambdarank conditioned on the user's risk score (advanced). Target = quintile rank of forward 12-month return inside the cluster.

STEP 3

Cap and pick

Map the user's questionnaire to a 0..1 risk score. Pick clusters by risk. Take the top-N reranked stocks subject to the 30% per-sector hard cap. Sector preferences act as a soft tiebreaker.

User questionnaire to risk score

risk_score = 0.25 * min(years_experience, 20) / 20
           + 0.25 * min(horizon_years, 20) / 20
           + 0.40 * min(drawdown_tolerance, 0.50) / 0.50
           + 0.10 * (1 if monthly_budget >= 500 else 0.5)
# clipped to [0, 1]; <0.33 -> calmest cluster, 0.33-0.66 -> calmer half, >0.66 -> volatile half

Baseline pipeline

  • K-Means on standardised (ann_return, ann_vol, sharpe, beta, max_drawdown).
  • Cluster centroids labelled with a heuristic rule set (defensive-low-beta, quality-compounders, growth-cyclical, high-risk-tail).
  • Within-cluster ranking by trailing Sharpe.
  • 30% per-sector cap; sector preferences soft-bumped to top of within-cluster ordering.

Advanced pipeline

  • Embedding. 4-layer fully-connected autoencoder, bottleneck 16, trained on the most recent 208 weekly returns per ticker.
  • Cluster. K-Means on (16-dim embedding + Sharpe + beta + max_drawdown).
  • Cluster-level mean-variance. PyPortfolioOpt's EfficientFrontier.max_sharpe with weight bounds (0, 0.30) per cluster.
  • Within-cluster ranker. LightGBM lambdarank with the user's risk score broadcast as a context feature; relevance label = quintile rank of forward 12-month return inside the cluster.
  • Inference. Same risk-driven cluster pick as the baseline; ranker score replaces trailing-Sharpe ordering; same 30% per-sector cap.

Discussion

What the cluster step buys

An end-to-end ranker on the full universe would assemble the eight highest-Sharpe stocks across 540 tickers, which would tend to concentrate in whichever sector ran hottest. The cluster-first decomposition forces the recommender to draw from multiple regions of the risk-return space, which is the operational form of diversification that Markowitz's covariance matrix originally encoded.

What the ranker buys

Trailing Sharpe is a reasonable scalar but ignores cross-sectional information that is well-documented to predict forward returns: short-term momentum, realised-volatility clustering, and the user-conditional context that the questionnaire provides. LightGBM lambdarank uses all three jointly and is trained against a ranking objective directly rather than a regression on forward return.

Limitations

  • Universe. ~540 tickers, US-large-cap-skewed; results do not generalise to small-caps, EM, or alt-assets.
  • Survivorship. Tickers that delisted between training window and now are absent, biasing the empirical forward-return distribution upward.
  • Deflated Sharpe. With multiple-testing correction (Harvey, Liu, Zhu 2015), small lifts of the advanced model over the baseline should not be over-interpreted.

Why no end-to-end model

End-to-end DRL or transformer rankers (AlphaStock, STHAN-SR) deliver higher NDCG on academic benchmarks but compromise on interpretability, sample efficiency on a 540-ticker universe, and the survivorship-corrected lift collapses to single digits. The cluster-then-rank decomposition is the right tradeoff for a beginner-investor recommender that has to surface a cluster label and per-stock risk metrics.

Conclusion

The Stock Portfolio Recommender decomposes into three interpretable steps - cluster the universe by risk-return profile, rank within cluster, map a questionnaire to a target risk that selects clusters - with a hard 30% per-sector cap. The baseline is K-Means on five hand-engineered features plus trailing Sharpe; the advanced pipeline replaces each step with a stronger learned component (autoencoder embeddings, mean-variance optimisation, LightGBM lambdarank). v1.0 ships code, implementation, references, manuscript, and presentation. v1.0 fills in the numerical results, runs the backtest, and ships the Streamlit demo described in the brief.
